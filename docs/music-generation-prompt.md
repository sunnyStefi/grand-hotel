Grand Hotel Gold — Music Generation Prompt
Brief
Generate an upbeat, looping chiptune track for a retro math-puzzle game about building a hotel empire. The music should feel like a jazzy, art-deco grand hotel in the 1980s — warm, glamorous, slightly ragtime-inspired, and instantly catchy for a 7-year-old player who'll hum it for weeks. Simple melody, calm vibes, not anxiety. Not too fast

Game Context
Grand Hotel Gold is a browser-based maze game where a bellhop drives through hotel corridors, solves addition problems to open doors, collects coins, and spends earnings to build a growing hotel. The core loop is fast, arcade-like, and reward-driven: drive → see a sum → steer into the correct answer → coins burst → repeat. The game session lasts 60–90 seconds per floor.

Music Requirements
Mood & Instrumentation:

Jazzy, upbeat, inviting — like a 1980s arcade with luxury hotel charm
Think ragtime swing meets chiptune nostalgia (Jazz Jackrabbit energy)
Instrumentation: classic 8-bit/16-bit synth palette (bright bassline, punchy drums, melodic synth lead with harmony)
Warm gold/red/teal color palette as sound (bright, friendly, never dark or chaotic)
Technical Specs:

Loop length: 8–16 bars (short, ~15–25 seconds) so it repeats seamlessly without feeling repetitive
Tempo: 110 BPM (energetic but not frenetic; he needs to think about math problems while playing)
Format: MP3 or WAV, loop-ready (ending ties smoothly back to the start)
Seamless looping: no fade-out; the last beat flows directly into the first without pause
Musical Character:

Melody: catchy and sing-able (he should be able to hum it after 10 minutes of play)
Rhythm: bouncy, syncopated — hints of ragtime or swing but very arcade-video-game
Harmonic color: major key or upbeat minor; no dissonance or tension (this is reward, not challenge)
Dynamics: bright and punchy; let drums and bass drive the groove without overwhelming the melody
Vibe Touchstones:

The joy of 1980s arcade games (Pac-Man, Dig Dug, Jazz Jackrabbit)
Elegance of a grand hotel lobby (think art-deco details, ding of a bell)
Pure, undiluted fun — no irony, no melancholy
Why This Matters
This track plays continuously during gameplay and is half the player's memory of the experience. It must feel rewarding just to listen to, making the whole game feel like a celebration. A mediocre loop will undercut even perfect gameplay. A great loop will make him ask for "one more floor" just to stay in that soundscape.
Sprinkle some chimes around.

Example Output

Upbeat bright synth melody (jazzy/swing inflection)
Clean, punchy drum kit (kick, snare, hihat)
Warm, rolling bassline (slightly syncopated)
Occasional harmony or flourish (organ stab, cowbell, xylophone accent) to add personality
No fade, no silence; loop restarts fluidly
The listener should think: "This is so happy, I want to play again."
